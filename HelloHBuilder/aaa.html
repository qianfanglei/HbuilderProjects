He's professional career:

    Golden State Warriors (2009–present)
  1. Early seasons (2009–11)
    Curry was selected with the seventh overall pick in the 2009 NBA draft by the Golden State Warriors.
  2. Injury-riddled year (2011–12)
    In May 2011, Curry had surgery on his right ankle to repair torn ligaments that were caused by multiple sprains from the season before.
  3. Getting back on track (2012–14)
    Prior to the start of the 2012–13 season, Curry agreed to a four-year, $44 million rookie scale contract extension with the Warriors.
  4. NBA championship and MVP (2014–15)
    Prior to the start of the 2014–15 season, the Warriors hired former NBA player and general manager Steve Kerr as their new head coach.
  5. Unanimous MVP (2015–16)
    In the Warriors' regular season finale on April 13 against the Memphis Grizzlies, Curry achieved another shooting milestone, becoming the first player to make 400 three-pointers in a season by knocking down 10 from long range on his way to 46 points and 402 total three-pointers. With a 125–104 win over the Grizzlies, the Warriors became the first 73-win team in NBA history, surpassing the 1995–96 Chicago Bulls' 72–10 record to finish the 2015–16 season with just nine losses.
